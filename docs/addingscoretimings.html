8 - Adding Judge Timings

In JDS, "judge timings" are the points in time during a song where the game tracks (and then scores) moves. You define these timings using code.

Tip

By default, score feedback will always show "X" during a test run. For a more realistic simulation, you can enable "randomize scoring?" in the Debug Tools sprite.

Aside from the basic "judge" blocks, there's a few more that you'll probably want to know how to use:

"Set gold move" marks the next scored move as a Gold Move. You can enter "1", "2", "3", or "4" to apply it to a specific coach, or "A" to apply it to all coaches at once.

"Start Combo Chance" is exactly what it sounds like. Combo Chance is a mechanic unique to Just Dance Scratchified. Official JDS songs always use it exactly once. It usually lasts for somewhere around 32 beats, and is set during a part of the routine that doesn't have any Gold Moves.

"Increase referencebeat" simply increases the reference beat for a specific coach without also scoring a move. (This is functionally the same as increasing the "delay" value in that coach's next judge block.) This can be useful for making yellow "repeat" blocks work correctly in your code.

Gold Move Effects​

Whenever a Gold Move occurs, you can add the Gold Move effect by inserting the beat number (whole or half) into your song's "Gold Move Occurrences" data list. The Gold Move will "explode" on that beat. This effect is purely aesthetic and doesn't affect scoring.
